BHSc Nutritional Medicine

Many people including myself believe in taking a holistic approach to nutrition. Holistic nutrition involves treating the whole person and includes all aspects of their being including their environment, sleep quality, stress levels, mental and emotional wellbeing, and of course the diet. It is amazing how all these components are closely intertwined and how dietary changes may have a large impact on the quality of life. Everyone is unique, otherwise we would all look and feel the same! This means that everyone’s nutritional requirements are unique too.

About Me

About Me

I was born in Perth and started dancing at a very young age. I attended a dancing school for 15 years, it was my second home. My childhood was a happy one and I cannot imagine growing up without the opportunity to dance!

The life skills I learnt through dancing have helped me in all aspects of my life. At a very young age I learnt the invaluable skills of discipline, team work, perseverance and dedication.

I developed an interest in Nutrition after suffering debilitating anxiety and panic attacks as a teenager. At 17 years old, I resigned from my full-time job due to these issues and had to find a way to manage my path towards adulthood. At the time, I was encouraged to take prescription medication, however I knew there had to be a more permanent solution. Through learning about nutrition, changes in my diet played a large role in overcoming any problems during this period of my life and I am grateful that I was able to accomplish this without medication.

In my mid 20’s, I experienced chronic fatigue syndrome. This changed my nutritional requirements and forced me to step back and look at my lifestyle. I had to learn that it’s not always healthy to try and push yourself to your limits; you are given one body and one chance to treat it the best you are able to.

I am constantly learning about my own body’s limits and working on ways to improve.

What I Offer

Nutrition should be simple, convenient and effective. This does not mean that we all need to be a MasterChef in the kitchen, and I certainly don’t want to spend hours each day preparing meals. Part of developing a nutrition plan is incorporating changes into your current lifestyle.

I wish that nutritional education was a larger part of our base education at school. Nutrition classes would teach you about your own body and how to live a healthy life; what could be more valuable? When your health declines, everything else seems to become less important.

Education is the launching pad I use during my consultations. If you are able to understand the way your body is working and how the food is working in your body, then you will be able to make informed decisions about your diet.

The aim is to find the cause of your symptoms, and by treating the cause, the symptoms may diminish. Some medical models currently treat the presenting symptoms and therefore the root cause is never identified and given the attention it requires to be resolved. All the systems in the body are connected and this is why a holistic approach is vital for a successful plan!
